And luckily, Stephen and Sara are both brave people. Now, I know Sara has the well-earned reputation of leaping into the unknown like a Patagonia-clad Xena Warrior Princess, but Stephen’s been brave for a long time too. In middle school, he didn’t take the easy, predictable route and play the trumpet or drums, Stephen chose to play the clarinet. He followed that up by quitting soccer to join the high school Speech Team and perform in plays. This is a man who has long had the conviction and confidence to lead his own life. So it’s no surprise that he took a chance to follow Sara up to Portland. Or that they now share a home, a dog, and an office. It’s been obvious for a long time that you two have faith in yourselves, and it’s beautiful to see that you have faith in each other. And you’re about to embark on the biggest adventure of them all—your honeymoon trip up Mt Everest!—just kidding, I’m talking about marriage. And in marriage, like any other adventure, you don’t know exactly how things are gonna go. There are gonna be whole seasons to celebrate as you continue building a life together—and oh yeah, there are gonna be simmering car arguments.
